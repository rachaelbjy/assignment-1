Materials Matter

Terracotta
Unlike plastic, unglazed terracotta is porous. It pulls moisture out of the soil, preventing the dreaded root rot.
Pumice
A lightweight volcanic rock that traps air and water in its microscopic pores, keeping soil airy and preventing compaction.
Precision Tools
Long tweezers and narrow trowels allow you to weed and repot without damaging the plant's delicate farina or getting spiked by glochids.

Equipment FAQ

Do I need special soil?

Yes! Regular potting soil holds too much moisture. Desert plants require a gritty mix of at least 50-70% inorganic material like pumice or perlite.

Why a long-spout can?

A long spout allows you to deliver water directly to the soil without getting the leaves wet, which prevents sunburn and fungal diseases.

How do I clean my shears?

Always wipe down your pruning shears with rubbing alcohol between cuts. This prevents the spread of bacteria from one plant to another.
